# Dairy-Free Stuffed Peppers

|               |   |
|---------------|---|
| Rating        | 4 |
| Ease          | 3 |
| Quickness     | 3 |
| Affordability | 5 |

Colorful bell peppers stuffed with a savory rice and vegetable filling. A hearty, satisfying dinner that's naturally dairy-free and packed with flavor.

# Ingredients

|Ingredient|Amount|Notes|
|-|-|-|
|Bell Peppers|4-6 large|Any color - red, yellow, green|
|Ground Turkey or Plant-Based Alternative|1 lb|For Mom, use plant-based crumbles or lentils|
|Brown Rice|1 cup uncooked|Cooked (makes about 3 cups cooked)|
|Onion|1 medium|Diced|
|Garlic|3-4 cloves|Minced|
|Carrots|2|Diced|
|Celery|2 stalks|Diced|
|Diced Tomatoes|1 can (15 oz)|With juice|
|Tomato Paste|2 tbsp||
|Italian Seasoning|1 tbsp|Dried herbs|
|Olive Oil|2 tbsp||
|Salt and Pepper||To taste|
|Vegetable Broth|1/2 cup|Optional, for extra moisture|

# Directions

- Preheat oven to 375Â°F
- Cook rice according to package directions and set aside
- Cut tops off peppers and remove seeds/membranes
- Lightly blanch peppers in boiling water for 3-4 minutes to soften, then drain
- Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat
- Add onion, carrots, and celery, cook until softened (about 5 minutes)
- Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant
- Add ground turkey/plant-based alternative and cook until browned
- Stir in diced tomatoes (with juice), tomato paste, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper
- Add cooked rice and vegetable broth if using, stir to combine
- Stuff mixture into prepared peppers
- Place peppers in a baking dish, cover with foil
- Bake for 30-35 minutes until peppers are tender
- Remove foil for last 10 minutes to lightly brown the tops

# Other Considerations

For extra nutrition, add spinach or zucchini to the filling. Can make filling ahead and assemble when ready to bake. Leftovers reheat well. For a vegetarian version, replace meat with more vegetables or beans. Great way to use up leftover rice.
