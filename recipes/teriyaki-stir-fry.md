# Teriyaki Stir Fry

|               |   |
|---------------|---|
| Rating        | 4 |
| Ease          | 4 |
| Quickness     | 4 |
| Affordability | 4 |

A sweeter stir-fry variation that kids usually love. Can use fresh or frozen vegetables.

# Ingredients

|Ingredient|Amount|Notes|
|-|-|-|
|Protein|1-1.5 lbs|Chicken, pork, or pre-cooked|
|Broccoli|1 head|Cut into florets|
|Bell Peppers|2|Cut into strips|
|Carrots|2-3|Cut into matchsticks or coins|
|Onion|1|Sliced|
|Garlic|2-3 cloves|Minced|
|Ginger|1 inch|Minced or grated|
|Soy Sauce|3-4 tbsp||
|Brown Sugar|2 tbsp|Or honey|
|Rice Vinegar|1 tbsp||
|Cornstarch|1 tbsp|For sauce thickening|
|Oil||
|Rice||For serving|

# Directions

## Sauce
- Mix soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, and cornstarch in a bowl
- Set aside

## Stir Fry
- Cook rice according to package directions
- Heat wok or large pan over high heat
- Add oil, then add protein and cook until done, remove and set aside
- Add more oil if needed, then add vegetables
- Stir-fry vegetables until crisp-tender
- Add garlic and ginger, cook for 30 seconds
- Return protein to pan
- Add sauce, stir until thickened and everything is coated
- Serve over rice

# Other Considerations

Can use frozen stir-fry vegetable mix for even faster prep. The sauce is very forgiving - adjust sweetness or saltiness to taste. Can add pineapple chunks for extra kid appeal.
