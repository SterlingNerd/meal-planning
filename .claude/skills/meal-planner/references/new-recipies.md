# Generating new recipies
Wen asked to generate new recipies:

- Assume we're generating dinner recipies unless otherwise specified.
- Aim for variety across the week (different proteins, cuisines, cooking methods)
- Balance quick meals with more involved ones based on schedule
- Consider prep work that can be done ahead (pre-cooked proteins, chopped veggies)
- Account for dietary restrictions when planning (especially Mom's red meat preference)
- Recipes should be kid-friendly, but we also want to expose her to a wide variety.

# Style
We don't need exact recipes. We usually cook by theme and feel, adapting ingredients and quantities based on what's available and our preferences. Recipes serve as guides and inspiration rather than strict instructions.

# Food Restrictions

- **Mostly Dairy Free**: Meals must not contain most dairy. Butter is ok, but milk and cheese are not. Substitutes are acceptable.
- **No Avocados**: Avocados must be avoided.
- **Modest refined Carbs**: While not a strict ban, refined carbs and added sugar should be kept low
- **Avoid Sea Food**: Our child will not eat seafood. We'll need alternatives for her if we are making a seafood dish.
- **Minimize Red Meat for Mom**: Red meat is fine occasionally, but not every day. Typically we cook an alternative protein for Mom.

# Recipe Format

All recipes are stored in the `recipes/` directory and follow a consistent format:

- **Title**: Recipe name
- **Rating Table**: Four metrics on a 1-5 scale:
  - Rating: Overall family enjoyment/appeal
  - Ease: Difficulty/complexity of preparation
  - Quickness: Time to prepare and cook
  - Affordability: Cost of ingredients
- **Description** (optional): Brief notes about the dish
- **Ingredients Table**: List of ingredients with amounts and notes
- **Directions**: Step-by-step cooking instructions
- **Other Considerations** (optional): Additional notes, substitutions, or variations

See `recipes/fake-fredo.md` for an example of our flexible, theme-based approach.
